A. BACKGROUND AND SIGNIFICANCE OF THE STUDY

The value of composting to communities and the environment may be aspects such as making plants thrive better, and be more healthy. With composting, our environment and plants will become healthier, which will allow it to become more plentiful and bountiful. With this newly found abundance of harvest, the communities will benefit and find value out of it as well; by creating employment opportunities, and of course a source of food and agriculture.



B. STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM

How will Phosphorous (Egg shells & Banana peels) compost affect the production of a Peanut plant?



C. Hypothesis

If the Phosphorus compost is mixed with the soil then the peanut plant will produce more pods.



D. Review of the Related Literature


Through research, we found that our plant, the peanut plant, heavily relies on composting for a healthy and bountiful harvest. The main nutrients that the peanut plant mostly requires is phosphorus, calcium, and potassium, all which are found in egg shells and banana peels. Calcium is essential for the peanut plant as it is what is responsible for holding both the cell walls, and the membrane within the plant cells. Calcium also acts as an intracellular messenger. Potassium is required for the plant as it allows the movement of water, nutrients, and carbohydrates within the plant tissue. Potassium is also involved with the enzyme activation in the plant, which in return affects the plant’s starch, protein, and ATP production, which is what regulates the plant’s rate of photosynthesis. These nutrients will help in the solving of our problem in wanting to affect our production of peanut pods.

EXPERIMENTAL DATA TO BE MEASURED OR OBSERVED

Experimental Measurement or Observation Description of Measurement or Observation Significance of Observation
Percentage of Calcium in compost One of the dependent variables; a measurement related to plant growth rate The compost sample will be tested for %Ca after a 4-week period of composting, 3 samples every composting set-up will be tested.
No. of leaves of plant The number of leaves on the plant will be counted every 3 days. The amount of leaves can help determine the plant’s growth rate.
Length of stem of plant The length of the largest stem will be measured every 3 days. The length of the stem can help determine the plant’s growth rate.
Health of the leaves The ratio of healthy to dead leaves will be calculated approximately every 3 days. By finding the ratio of healthy to dead leaves, we can find the amount of nutrients the plant is receiving from the compost. Is it lacking, is it sufficient?
No. of flowers blossomed The number of flowers blossomed will be counted every 3 days. Seeing how fast the flowers blossom can help determine the plant’s growth rate and peanut production.
No. of seeds planted The number of seeds planted into the ground will be counted 3 days after each count of the flowers bloomed. Seeing how fast the flowers plant themselves into the soil after being blossomed, can help determine the efficiency of the peanut production from the plant.
